# Product Brief

## Problem

AI moves too fast. New models, papers, tools, open-source projects, products,
benchmarks, and industry changes appear every day.

The problem is not only missing information. The deeper problem is being pulled
by noisy summaries, hype, and personal feeds without knowing what is actually
important, what is reliable, and what deserves study or practice.

## Target User

A personal AI learner and practitioner who wants a broad AI radar first, then
personalized recommendations second.

The user wants to know what is changing across the whole AI landscape: frontier
models, research, agents, RAG, multimodal AI, AI coding, developer tools,
infrastructure, model deployment, evals, benchmarks, safety, policy, open
source, products, business adoption, and China/global ecosystem signals.

The current focus is Browser Use and AI Agent, but the radar should not be
limited to that topic. Next week the focus may be RAG, multimodal AI, evals,
AI coding, model deployment, or another area. Focus changes the advice, not the
collection boundary.

## Job To Be Done

When I check AI updates, I want a comprehensive, evidence-aware radar that
helps me understand what changed across AI, how reliable each source is, what
matters broadly, and what I should learn or try next given my current focus.

## Product Promise

Keep me standing in the full AI frontier, then use source quality and my
current focus to convert important signals into learning, verification, and
practice.

## Non-Goals

- Do not become a generic AI hot list.
- Do not optimize for virality or click-through.
- Do not treat KOL opinions as facts.
- Do not summarize every source.
- Do not become a narrow Browser Use, Agent, RAG, or AI coding digest.
- Do not force every signal into the current learning focus.
- Do not use browser automation when stable structured access is better.

## Success Criteria

- Daily radar can be read in less than 10 minutes.
- Weekly learning map identifies a small number of real study directions.
- The radar covers the major AI map, not only the current focus.
- Every important item keeps original source links.
- Each item separates fact, interpretation, uncertainty, and suggested action.
- Recommendations explain whether they are driven by broad importance, source
  reliability, current focus fit, or hands-on value.
- High global importance items are not filtered out just because they are not
  related to the current focus.
- Current focus items receive enough attention to support hands-on practice.
